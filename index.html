About Me
000 /

The dawn of my college experience began on a pre-med track, a path I initially believed was my calling. However, by the end of my freshman year, a moment of introspection led me to a realization: I didn't enjoy chemistry nor did I actually want to go to medical school.

As I went back to the chalk board, I enrolled in a sociology class titled "The Politics of Global Health" to satisfy my interest in medicine without focusing on chemical and biological processes that a medical student otherwise would. This course unveiled a unique lens through which to view diseases, a perspective that was vastly different from the lens of my chemistry professor. It wasn't just about molecular interactions and chemical reactions; it was more holistic, referencing societal implications, power dynamics, and the broader and complex tapestry of health concerns across social spheres. After sophomore year, my academic canvas was richly colored with the diverse shades of humanities and profound insights from my sociology coursework. Amid this epiphany, I felt a desire for a technical toolkit that could bolster and enhance my understanding of societal complexities.

This search for depth led me to the Data Science department at W&M. Recognizing the promising confluence between structured data analytics and social contextual understanding from sociology, I proudly declared a double major of Data Science and Sociology.
Today, as I stand at this compelling crossroads of sociology and data analytics, I am driven by an ambition: to blend the precision of data with the richness of human narratives, striving for insights that bridge the vast chasm between numbers and nuanced societal stories.
